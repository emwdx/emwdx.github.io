Rubric Scale 1

Health Interpersonal Communication

Competency: Interpersonal communication skills to enhance health

Developing (2.0)

Vague or Passive Messaging

• Key concepts (consent, boundaries) mentioned but not actively modeled in roleplay

• Simplistic, one-sided conflict — no negotiation, empathy, or refusal skills shown

• Advice inadvertently makes situation worse or falls into "vague & passive" quadrant

Proficient (3.0) Target

Assertive & Respectful

• Characters clearly and assertively state needs, boundaries, and wants (non-aggressive)

• Active dialogue respects the feelings, needs, and safety of all parties

• Final message lands in "Clear & Assertive / Promotes Caring Decisions" quadrant

• Demonstrates refusal skills, negotiation, or conflict resolution applicable to HS

Exemplary (4.0)

Empowerment & Nuance

• Considers multiple sides of conflict — illustrates complexity (e.g., negotiating when feelings are hurt)

• Models peer-to-peer support: characters help others develop self-advocacy skills

• Communication strategies strong enough to serve as a Nido community training resource

Rubric Scale 2

Health: Comprehensive Sexuality Education

Competency: Students comprehend functional health knowledge

Currently Awarded:
N/A
Developing (2.0)

Basic Identification Only

• Identifies a scenario but fails to link it to healthy decision-making or behavioral consequences

• Contains minor inaccuracies about consent or boundaries

• Portrays unhealthy relationship behaviors (e.g., digital tracking) as "normal" or "no big deal"

Proficient (3.0) Target

Value-Based Decisions

• Analyzes how personal values, societal expectations, and digital experiences influence teen relationships

• Empowers Nido HS peers to protect their own physical/emotional health and care for others

• Addresses an authentic, localized scenario (phone privacy, Find My, consent at parties) with maturity

Exemplary (4.0)

Systemic & Cultural Context

• Explicitly connects scenario (e.g., Find My) to systemic trust, digital intimacy, safety, or emotional abuse dynamics

• Incorporates diverse viewpoints from empathy interviews or peer research to address generational/cultural differences

Rubric Scale 3

Technology: Creative Communicator

Competency: Choose a platform or tool to craft & convey a message

Currently Awarded:
N/A
Developing (2.0)

Production Pitfalls

• Shaky footage, portrait/vertical framing, inaudible dialogue, or abrupt visual cuts

• Video significantly shorter than 0:45 or longer than 1:00, weakening PSA structure

• Visuals or text feel generic and uncustomized for Nido High School peers

Proficient (3.0) Target

Technical Fundamentals

• Strictly Landscape framing, Rule of Thirds composition, stable/steady camera throughout

• Good lighting; clear, consistent audio — music (if used) does not drown out voices

• Exactly 0:45–1:00 with clean cuts, intentional title screens, and/or text graphics

• All music/SFX/b-roll comply with copyright (Creative Commons or royalty-free via CapCut)

Exemplary (4.0)

Cinematic Master

• Intentional pacing, creative transitions, and color correction that heightens emotional impact

• Evidence of intentional editing choices to avoid a "preachy" tone (e.g., soundtrack levels, graphics)

• Unique, professional "director's voice" that feels authentic to teenage media consumption
